## 1. Legislative Foundation

### 1.1 The Governing Statutes

The Marshall Islands DAO LLC framework rests on four primary pieces of legislation:

| Statute | Year | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Non-Profit Entities (Amendment) Act** | 2021 | Inaugural Web3 legislation. Established non-profit DAO LLCs. Eliminated requirements to disclose all member identities, mandated managers, and local bank accounts. |
| **Decentralized Autonomous Organization Act** | 2022 (Nov 25) | The core DAO Act. First-in-the-world legislation legally recognizing DAOs as domestic LLCs with full legal personhood. |
| **DAO Act Amendments** | 2023 (Oct) | Introduced for-profit DAO LLCs, Series DAO LLCs, governance token securities exemption, open-source software liability immunity, and accelerated registration (max 30 days). |
| **DAO Regulations** | 2024 | Streamlined registration procedures, documentation requirements, beneficial ownership reporting, and compliance enforcement. |

All DAO LLCs are also subject to the underlying **Limited Liability Company Act** (Title 52 MIRC Chapter 4), which is modeled on Delaware LLC law. The Marshall Islands has written into law that it follows Delaware precedent unless there is a conflicting RMI precedent or law.

### 1.2 Key Provisions of the DAO Act (2022)

The original Act provides:

- **Legal Personhood**: DAOs gain separate legal entity status. They can own property, enter contracts, engage service providers, sue and be sued in their own names.
- **LLC Integration**: DAOs incorporate under the internationally recognized LLC structure, designated as "DAO LLC."
- **Smart Contract Recognition**: The certificate of formation or LLC agreement must include a publicly available identifier of any smart contract directly used to manage the DAO.
- **Operating Agreement Flexibility**: The DAO's operating agreement may consist partly or wholly of smart contracts.
- **No Board Required**: DAOs are not required to have directors, officers, trustees, supervisors, or managers.
- **Blockchain Records**: Written or paper records are not required if they are maintained on the blockchain.
- **Privacy**: Except for one identified person (the UBO), every member of the DAO may remain anonymous.
- **Training Fund**: Established a fund for the RMI government to continue education and training around DAOs.
- **Management Modes**: DAOs must designate as either "member-managed" or "algorithmically managed."

### 1.3 2023 Amendments

The 2023 amendments ("the most comprehensive law for DAOs globally" per MIDAO) added:

- **For-Profit DAO LLCs**: Expanded beyond the original non-profit-only structure to allow for-profit entities with member distributions.
- **Series DAO LLCs**: Enabled sub-DAOs with separate assets and liabilities within a single parent LLC.
- **Governance Token Clarification**: Explicitly states that "a governance token conferring no economic rights shall not be deemed a security."
- **Open-Source Software Immunity**: DAOs receive explicit protection from liability for open-source software they create, even if others misuse it.
- **Accelerated Registration**: Reduced maximum processing time from 30-60 days to a maximum of 30 days.
- **Additional Protections**: Enhanced liability shield provisions and operational protections for members.

### 1.4 2024 Regulations

The 2024 DAO Regulations complemented the existing framework by:

- Streamlining registration procedures and documentation requirements.
- Formalizing beneficial ownership reporting obligations (Section 15 empowers the licensing authority to cancel certificates of formation for non-compliance).
- Establishing formal compliance and enforcement mechanisms.
- Codifying the annual reporting framework.

### 1.5 Legal Hierarchy (Conflict Resolution)

The Act creates an explicit hierarchy of legal application when conflicts arise between sources of authority:

1. **DAO Act and RMI law** (highest priority)
2. **Conventional LLC law** (underlying LLC Act)
3. **Written Operating Agreement**
4. **Smart Contract Code** (lowest priority)

This means: if smart contract code contradicts the written operating agreement, the operating agreement prevails. If the operating agreement conflicts with RMI LLC law, the law prevails. This is critical for ensuring legal certainty while still allowing on-chain governance.

## 2. Entity Types: Non-Profit vs For-Profit

### 2.1 Non-Profit DAO LLC

**Tax Status**: Completely tax-free. Zero corporate income tax, zero capital gains tax, zero withholding tax, no regular filing requirements beyond annual reporting.

**Constraints**:
- No part of the income or profit is distributable to members, directors, or officers.
- Prohibited from engaging in propaganda, legislative influence (with limited exceptions), or political campaign participation.
- Authorized to pay only reasonable compensation for services and expense reimbursement.
- The entity may hold treasury assets and make investments; it simply cannot distribute profits to members.

**Securities Treatment**: All digital assets including NFTs issued by a non-profit DAO LLC shall not be deemed a digital security (automatic exemption).

**Best For**: Open-source protocols, public goods, grants programs, governance DAOs, community-owned infrastructure.

### 2.2 For-Profit DAO LLC

**Tax Status**: Subject to **3% Gross Revenue Tax (GRT)** on earned income and interest. Does NOT apply to capital gains and dividends. No pass-through taxation to individual members (unlike US LLCs).

**Capabilities**:
- Can distribute profits to members.
- Can issue tokens with economic rights.
- Must file annual financial reporting.
- Subject to revenue reporting requirements.

**Securities Treatment**: Tokens with economic rights are NOT automatically classified as securities, but are also NOT automatically exempt. Each situation requires individual analysis under RMI securities law. However, RMI securities laws only apply if tokens are sold to Marshall Islands residents.

**Best For**: Revenue-generating protocols, DeFi platforms, tokenized investment vehicles, play-to-earn gaming ecosystems.

### 2.3 Key Differences Summary

| Feature | Non-Profit | For-Profit |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Taxation | Zero | 3% GRT (excl. cap gains/dividends) |
| Profit Distribution | Prohibited | Allowed |
| Securities Exemption | Automatic for all tokens | Case-by-case analysis |
| Financial Reporting | Minimal | Annual required |
| Purpose Statement | Must connect to non-profit activity | Flexible |
| Treasury Holdings | Allowed (no distribution) | Allowed (with distribution) |

## 3. Series DAO LLC

### 3.1 Overview

Introduced by the 2023 amendments, Series DAO LLCs allow the creation of multiple, distinct series within a single LLC, each with its own:

- **Separate assets** — each series has its own treasury/property
- **Separate liabilities** — liabilities of one series do NOT affect other series or the parent LLC
- **Separate governance structures** — each series can have its own management model
- **Separate membership** — distinct member composition per series

### 3.2 How Series DAO LLCs Work

Each series functions as a legally separate entity within the parent DAO LLC. The parent LLC creates "child" series that operate independently. The liability of one series is ring-fenced, meaning creditors of Series A cannot reach the assets of Series B or the parent.

### 3.3 Use Cases

- **Multi-project DAOs**: Segregate different projects within one entity (e.g., a protocol with separate lending, DEX, and NFT divisions).
- **Risk Isolation**: Silo high-risk activities (e.g., experimental DeFi strategies) away from stable operations.
- **Investment DAOs**: Create separate investment vehicles per fund vintage or strategy.
- **Sub-DAO Structures**: Formalize sub-DAO relationships with legal asset/liability separation.
- **Grant Programs**: Isolate grant-making activities from operational treasury.

### 3.4 Comparison with Traditional Structures

Previously, crypto projects had to create dual entities (e.g., a BVI company + Cayman Foundation) to achieve asset/liability separation. A single RMI Series DAO LLC can achieve the same separation without creating multiple entities, greatly reducing cost and complexity.

## 4. Smart Contract as Membership Registry

### 4.1 Legal Recognition

The Marshall Islands DAO Act explicitly recognizes smart contracts as the authoritative membership record. Key provisions:

- **Membership on-chain**: "Membership in the DAO LLC can be based on the token holding criterion and tracked on-chain."
- **Token-based membership**: Token holders automatically become LLC members without requiring separate onboarding or KYC procedures (below the 25% governance threshold).
- **Simultaneous transfer**: Membership transfers occur simultaneously with token transfers on-chain.
- **Operating agreement integration**: "Members' ownership of such a company may be defined in such a plain document as the register of members AND in the company's smart contract."

### 4.2 Smart Contract Requirements

The formation documents must include:

1. **Publicly available identifier** of any smart contract directly used to manage the DAO (e.g., the contract address on the relevant blockchain).
2. **Smart contract summary**: Technical description of autonomous operations and on-chain logic.
3. **Governance model designation**: Whether "member-managed" or "algorithmically managed."

### 4.3 What the Smart Contract Must Address

Based on the Odos DAO LLC operating agreement (a real-world example), the smart contract referenced in the operating agreement should:

- Define the token that confers membership (e.g., specific ERC-20 contract address on a specific blockchain).
- Establish voting rights proportional to token holdings (one vote per governance token).
- Support voting delegation mechanisms.
- Define quorum and approval thresholds.
- Specify proposal types and their required approval percentages.
- Handle member dissociation (automatic when token balance reaches zero).

### 4.4 On-Chain Member Registry

The smart contract serves as the **on-chain Member Registry**. Per the Odos DAO LLC precedent:

- The current member registry requirement is "satisfied by on-chain Member Registry."
- The blockchain record is the authoritative source for membership status.
- No duplicate off-chain membership registry is required.
- Formation documents, amendments, operating agreement, financial statements, and voting records must still be maintained (can be on-chain or off-chain at the entity's discretion).

### 4.5 Multi-Chain Considerations

The law does not restrict smart contracts to any specific blockchain. Current implementations include:

- **Ethereum**: Odos DAO LLC uses Ethereum-based ERC-20 tokens.
- **Solana**: Pyth DAO LLC's operating agreement points to Pyth governance smart contracts (Solana-native).
- **Any blockchain**: The law is chain-agnostic; the key requirement is a "publicly available identifier."

## 5. Governance Models

### 5.1 Member-Managed

Members retain direct control over the DAO's operations and decisions. Governance is exercised through:

- Token-weighted voting (one vote per governance token).
- One-member-one-vote basis (alternative to token-weighted).
- On-chain proposals and voting.
- Delegation of voting power to other members.

### 5.2 Algorithmically Managed

Governance is exercised by the DAO's smart contracts through:

- Pre-programmed rules and conditions encoded in smart contracts.
- Automated execution of governance decisions.
- No requirement for human managers, directors, or officers.

### 5.3 Hybrid Governance

In practice, most DAOs use a hybrid approach. The Odos DAO LLC example demonstrates:

- **On-chain governance**: Token voting for proposals via defined governance programs.
- **Special Delegates**: Named individuals with specific administrative powers (managing property, finances, AML compliance, binding the company within delegated scope).
- **Councils**: Optional subgroups with specific powers defined by governance charters.

### 5.4 No Director/Officer Requirement

In its baseline setup, an RMI DAO LLC has no directors or officers. This is a critical differentiator from:

- **Cayman Islands Foundations**: Require directors who must sign off on every decision.
- **BVI Foundations**: Require an independent supervisor/director.
- **Swiss Associations**: Require a board and officers.
- **Wyoming DUNAs**: Have certain structural requirements.

The RMI allows optional appointment of managers/officers but never mandates them, enabling true decentralization.

### 5.5 Implied Covenant of Good Faith

The DAO Act imposes an implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing, with no explicit fiduciary duty beyond what is specified in governing documents. Per the Odos DAO LLC operating agreement: "this Agreement is not intended to, and does not, create or impose any fiduciary duty on any Member...each Member hereby waives...any and all fiduciary duties."

Members are bound only by expressed contractual obligations and the implied covenant of good faith.

## 6. Liability Protection

### 6.1 Limited Liability Shield

The DAO LLC provides full limited liability protection:

- **No member shall be liable as such for the liabilities of the Company**, regardless of formality failures.
- Members are not personally liable for DAO debts or legal actions.
- The LLC's debts and obligations are separate from members' personal assets.
- Creditors of the LLC cannot reach members' personal assets.
- Members' liabilities to the LLC are limited to their capital contribution (if any).

### 6.2 Delaware Precedent

Because RMI corporate law follows Delaware precedent (unless conflicting RMI law exists), the liability shield benefits from over a century of Delaware LLC case law and jurisprudence. This provides:

- Well-established veil-piercing standards.
- Predictable judicial interpretation.
- International recognition of the corporate form.

### 6.3 Corporate Veil Protection

The LLC structure provides a "corporate veil" separating the entity from its members. This veil can only be pierced under limited circumstances (following Delaware precedent):

- **Commingling of funds**: Mixing personal and entity assets.
- **Inadequate capitalization**: Entity has insufficient assets relative to its obligations.
- **Fraud or illegality**: Entity is used as a vehicle for fraud.
- **Alter ego**: Entity is merely an alter ego of a controlling person (no actual separate existence).
- **Failure to observe formalities**: Not maintaining the LLC as a separate entity (though the Act is lenient here for DAOs).

### 6.4 Open-Source Software Immunity (2023)

The 2023 amendments added explicit protection: DAOs receive immunity from liability for any open-source software they create, even if third parties misuse it. This addresses a major legal concern in DeFi, where protocol developers might otherwise be held liable for exploits or misuse.

### 6.5 Comparison: Unregistered DAO Risk

Without legal wrapping, DAOs face catastrophic liability exposure. The **Ooki DAO case** (CFTC v. Ooki DAO, 2022) demonstrated that members of unregistered DAOs can be held personally liable with "joint and several liability" for all DAO-related activities. The RMI DAO LLC eliminates this risk.

### 6.6 Restricted Person Provisions

Members are automatically dissociated (lose membership) if they are classified as a "Restricted Person":

- Individuals on FATF, UN Security Council, or HMT sanctions lists.
- Those subject to sanctions by US, EU, HMT, FATF, UN, or other applicable authorities.
- Those violating adopted AML policies.

## 7. Formation Process

### 7.1 Step-by-Step Formation

**Step 1: Community Discussion and Decision**
- Internal DAO dialogue through AMAs, forums, Discord, or governance proposals.
- Gauge member interest and vote on incorporation (if existing DAO).
- New projects: Founding team decides on structure.

**Step 2: Select Entity Type**
- Choose between Non-Profit DAO LLC or For-Profit DAO LLC.
- Determine governance model: member-managed or algorithmically managed.
- Decide if Series DAO LLC structure is needed.

**Step 3: Select and Reserve a Name**
- Choose a unique DAO LLC name.
- Name must include "DAO LLC" designation.
- Submit for approval by the Registrar.

**Step 4: KYC and Compliance**
- At least one incorporator required (can be an individual or company; need not be a resident or member).
- Founders must supply KYC: proof of identity (nonexpired passport) and proof of residential address.
- At least one Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) must be identified and undergo KYC screening.
- Members with 25%+ governance rights must also complete KYC.
- All other members may remain anonymous.

**Step 5: Draft Constitutional Documents**
Three essential documents are required:

1. **Certificate of Formation**:
   - Statement designating the organization as a DAO.
   - Matters required by the LLC Act.
   - Publicly available identifier of any smart contract directly used to manage the DAO.
   - Entity name with DAO LLC designation.

2. **Operating Agreement**:
   - Purpose of the DAO.
   - Rights and duties of members.
   - Entitlements and obligations.
   - Dispute resolution methods.
   - Liability limitations.
   - Smart contract frameworks and amendment protocols.
   - Voting procedures and vote counting.
   - Treasury creation and management.
   - Additional governing bodies (if any).
   - May consist partly or wholly of smart contracts.

3. **Charter / Articles of Association**:
   - Defines governance model and operational logic.
   - Member responsibilities.
   - Smart contract summary (technical description of on-chain logic).

**Step 6: Appoint a Registered Agent**
- A registered agent within the Marshall Islands is legally required.
- MIDAO serves as the exclusive registered agent for DAO LLCs (public-private partnership with RMI government).
- No local office or law firm is otherwise required.

**Step 7: Submit to Registrar**
- File Certificate of Formation, Operating Agreement, and Foreign Investment Business License (FIBL) application with the Registrar of Corporations.
- Submit smart contract details and technical documentation.
- Pay filing fees.

**Step 8: Receive Certificate of Formation**
- Registrar reviews and issues the Charter/Certificate of Formation.
- Process typically takes 2-4 weeks (maximum 30 days by law).
- Entity becomes legally recognized upon issuance.

**Step 9: Post-Formation**
- Set up governance (deploy/reference smart contracts).
- Appoint Special Delegates or managers (optional).
- Establish banking relationships (optional).
- Begin operations.

### 7.2 Formation Requirements Summary

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Minimum members | No minimum (any person may form with one or more members) |
| Incorporator | At least one (individual or company, need not be resident) |
| UBO identification | At least one person must be named |
| KYC threshold | Members with 25%+ governance rights |
| Local office | Not required |
| Local directors | Not required |
| Local law firm | Not required |
| Local bank account | Not required |
| Minimum capital | None |
| Timeline | 2-4 weeks (max 30 days by law) |

## 8. Registered Agent Requirements

### 8.1 Legal Requirement

Every DAO LLC must maintain a registered office address and registered agent within the Marshall Islands continuously. The registered agent:

- Receives and handles government correspondence.
- Ensures annual compliance filings are submitted.
- Serves as the point of contact for the Registrar.
- Receives service of process on behalf of the entity.

### 8.2 MIDAO as Exclusive Agent

MIDAO operates as an exclusive **Public-Private Partnership** with the Republic of the Marshall Islands government:

- Exclusively authorized by the RMI government to process and manage DAO LLC registrations.
- Serves as the registered agent for all DAO LLCs.
- Operates the public DAO LLC registry (registry.midao.org).

### 8.3 Trust Company of the Marshall Islands (TCMI)

As of 2025, the Trust Company of the Marshall Islands (TCMI) holds exclusive authority over non-domestic company formations in RMI. TCMI now mandates pre-approval processes, particularly for VASP activities and token-related operations. TCMI can dissolve entities if registered agent services cease, effectively gating which companies can operate in the jurisdiction.

### 8.4 Alternative Providers

While MIDAO is the primary (and government-sanctioned) provider for DAO LLCs specifically, other service providers have offered RMI LLC formation services:

- **Offshore Companies International (OCI)**: Offers formation at $6,000 + $5,500 annual fees. Delivers Certificate of Formation, Constitution/Articles, Operating Agreement, FIBL template, and first year of registered agent/office service.
- **DAObox**: Offers wrapper design and governance integration starting at $17,500 all-inclusive (first year).
- **OtoCo**: Previously offered automated formation; now disabled for new RMI entities pending compliance procedures (as of 2025).
- **Otonomos**: Standalone RMI LLC formation at $550 one-time + $1,400 annual dues.
- **SB-SB**: Offers formation services starting around $15,000.

## 9. Compliance Requirements

### 9.1 Annual Reporting

**Deadline**: Between January 1st and March 31st each year.

**Content**: The annual report must contain:
- Beneficial ownership information (updated).
- Leadership/management details.
- Community engagement summary.
- Financial information (for-profit entities).
- Confirmation of operational status.
- Any structural changes during the year.

### 9.2 Beneficial Ownership Reporting

**Beneficial Owner Information Report (BOIR)** must identify each beneficial owner:

| Required Data | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| Full legal name | Complete name as on identity documents |
| Date of birth | DOB of beneficial owner |
| Address | Residential address |
| Nonexpired passport number | Government-issued ID |
| Wallet address(es) | All wallet addresses associated with the DAO |

**Beneficial Owner Definition**: A person who exercises control through:
- More than 25% of the LLC's interests or voting rights.
- Management of the LLC.
- Other forms of control.

**Update Requirement**: If BOIR information changes (e.g., a new key member joins, ownership threshold crosses 25%), the report must be updated promptly.

### 9.3 KYC Requirements (Tiered)

| Tier | Who | Requirement |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Founders/Incorporators | All founders | Full KYC (passport + proof of address) |
| 25%+ governance rights | Major token holders | Annual KYC renewal |
| 10%+ governance rights | Significant holders | KYC process with local regulator |
| Below 10% | Regular members | May remain anonymous |
| UBO | At least one person | Full identification and KYC screening |

KYC is conducted annually, typically in January.

### 9.4 AML/CTF Compliance

- Anti-Money Laundering regulations apply to virtual asset transfers exceeding USD 1,000.
- Cross-border transaction monitoring is required.
- Entities must adopt and comply with AML policies.
- Entities involved in custody of digital assets must comply with international AML/CTF standards.
- However, DAO LLCs themselves cannot engage in digital asset custody (no VASP license available).

### 9.5 Smart Contract Updates

Material updates to the DAO's operational smart contracts may require:
- Re-submission of technical documentation to the Registrar.
- Legal review to ensure continued compliance.
- Disclosure in the next annual report.

### 9.6 Corporate Continuation

Every year, the DAO LLC must complete **corporate continuation** to be renewed for the next year of operation. This involves:
- Filing annual reports.
- Paying government fees.
- Paying registered agent fees.
- Maintaining registered office and agent.

### 9.7 Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Daily non-compliance | USD 500 per day the violation continues |
| General fines | Up to USD 10,000 |
| Severe violations | Imprisonment possible |
| False information | Criminal penalties |
| Persistent non-compliance | Cancellation of certificate of formation (Section 15, 2024 Regulations) |

### 9.8 International Standards

The Marshall Islands:
- Follows international beneficial ownership standards.
- Is NOT on any tax evasion blacklists.
- Operates a mixed legal system combining U.S. and English common law.
- Has a Compact of Free Association (COFA) with the United States, renewed in 2024 for 20 years.

## 10. Taxation

### 10.1 Non-Profit DAO LLC

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Corporate income tax | 0% |
| Capital gains tax | 0% |
| Withholding tax | 0% |
| Pass-through to members | None |
| Regular tax filing | Not required |

### 10.2 For-Profit DAO LLC

| Tax Type | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Gross Revenue Tax (GRT) | 3% on earned revenue and interest |
| Capital gains | Excluded from GRT |
| Dividends | Excluded from GRT |
| Pass-through to members | None |
| Annual financial reporting | Required |

### 10.3 Key Tax Advantages

- **No pass-through taxation**: Unlike US LLCs (including Wyoming), profits are not attributed to individual members' personal income tax returns. This is a fundamental advantage for international DAOs.
- **No capital gains or dividend tax**: The 3% GRT for for-profit entities excludes the most common forms of crypto income.
- **No withholding tax**: Distributions to members are not subject to withholding.
- **Not on blacklists**: The Marshall Islands is not on the EU, OECD, or FATF tax haven blacklists, providing international credibility.

### 10.4 Member-Level Tax Considerations

While the Marshall Islands does not impose pass-through taxes, individual members remain subject to the tax laws of their own countries of residence. The entity-level tax treatment (or lack thereof) does not override members' personal tax obligations.

## 11. Securities Law Treatment

### 11.1 Governance Tokens (No Economic Rights)

**Clear exemption**: "A governance token conferring no economic rights shall not be deemed a security" under Marshall Islands law. This is a statutory safe harbor.

The distinction is critical: governance tokens are not securities "if they don't confer any economic rights, which is different from possible financial gain." A token holder may benefit from price appreciation while still having a governance token that does not confer economic rights (e.g., no dividend rights, no profit share, no liquidation preference).

### 11.2 Non-Profit DAO Tokens

**Automatic exemption**: "All digital assets including non-fungible tokens issued by a non-profit DAO LLC shall not be deemed a digital security." This is an absolute exemption for non-profit entities.

### 11.3 For-Profit Tokens with Economic Rights

Tokens conferring economic rights in for-profit structures lack automatic classification under Marshall Islands law. The DAO Act:
- Does NOT automatically exempt them.
- Does NOT automatically classify them as securities.
- Requires individual analysis on a case-by-case basis.

### 11.4 Territorial Application

**RMI securities laws only apply when tokens are sold to Marshall Islands residents.** The DAO Act exempts DAOs from the Marshall Islands Securities and Investment Act "to the extent that a DAO LLC is not issuing, selling, exchanging or transferring any digital securities to residents of the Republic."

Most countries' securities laws apply when tokens are sold within their borders, regardless of where the DAO is domiciled. The RMI exemption does not override foreign securities law.

### 11.5 Digital Securities Registration

If a DAO issues tokens that ARE classified as digital securities:
- Must register with RMI regulators.
- Must receive Cabinet approval.
- An exemption may be granted for securities issued by non-profit entities.

## 12. On-Chain Cap Table Legal Implications

### 12.1 RMI Framework for On-Chain Cap Tables

The Marshall Islands DAO Act is uniquely positioned for on-chain cap tables because:

- The smart contract IS the legally recognized membership registry.
- Token holdings define membership interests.
- No duplicate off-chain registry is required.
- Transfers on-chain automatically update membership status.
- The blockchain is the authoritative record of ownership.

### 12.2 Solana-Specific Considerations

For a Solana-based smart contract as the membership registry:

- The operating agreement must reference the specific Solana program address.
- The Pyth DAO LLC (one of the most prominent RMI DAO LLCs) already uses Solana-native governance smart contracts, establishing precedent.
- The token's SPL token address serves as the "publicly available identifier" required by the Act.
- Solana's high throughput enables real-time cap table updates with every token transfer.

### 12.3 SEC Framework for On-Chain Cap Tables (US Context)

In May 2025, the SEC acknowledged that a blockchain can serve as the official **Master Securityholder File** for a registered transfer agent:

- A registered transfer agent may use distributed ledger technology as its official Master Securityholder File.
- The transfer agent would NOT need to maintain a duplicate off-chain "digital twin."
- Personal information can be kept off-chain while transaction information stays on-chain.
- Records must be secure, accurate, up to date, and producible to the SEC in easily readable format.
- The format of issuance or technology used for recordkeeping (on-chain or off-chain) does not alter the application of federal securities laws.

**Landmark Precedent**: Galaxy Digital tokenized its SEC-registered common stock on Solana through Superstate in 2025, with 32,374 GLXY shares tokenized. This established the first live implementation of tokenized on-chain cap tables for SEC-registered securities.

### 12.4 Legal Disclosures Needed for On-Chain Cap Table

| Disclosure | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Smart contract address | Must be publicly identified in formation documents |
| Blockchain network | Must be specified (e.g., Solana mainnet) |
| Token standard | Must be documented (e.g., SPL Token) |
| Governance logic | Summary of on-chain governance mechanics |
| Membership criteria | How token holding confers membership |
| Transfer restrictions | Any programmatic transfer restrictions |
| Dissociation triggers | When token balance of zero triggers membership loss |
| Beneficial ownership | 25%+ holders must be identified to RMI regulators |
| Material updates | Smart contract upgrades must be disclosed |

### 12.5 Implications for Entity Formation Business

For building a legal entity formation business around on-chain cap tables:

1. **Template Operating Agreements**: Create templates that reference Solana program addresses and SPL token contracts.
2. **Smart Contract Audits**: Governance smart contracts should be audited to ensure they meet the requirements of the operating agreement.
3. **Dual Compliance**: If tokens may be securities in any jurisdiction, ensure compliance with both RMI law and the relevant foreign securities law.
4. **Transfer Agent Integration**: For SEC-registered securities, integrate with a registered transfer agent that supports Solana.
5. **Real-Time Compliance**: Build tooling to monitor the 25% governance threshold in real-time and trigger KYC requirements automatically.

## 13. Banking Access

### 13.1 Available Banking

RMI DAO LLCs have successfully opened accounts with:

- **Signature Bank** (New York City) — Admiralty LLC (Clipper Exchange) was among the first approved.
- **Western Alliance Bank**
- Other institutions (MIDAO makes introductions).

### 13.2 Challenges

- DAOs are in a higher risk bracket for banks, requiring stricter due diligence.
- Not every DAO will be approved; banking approval requires an in-depth risk management process.
- Some countries classify Marshall Islands as a high-risk jurisdiction, complicating transactions.
- The Marshall Islands has limited domestic banking infrastructure (several banks withdrew after the 2008 financial crisis).

### 13.3 No Local Banking Requirement

The DAO Act eliminated the requirement for a local Marshall Islands bank account, meaning entities can bank entirely through international institutions.

## 14. AI Agent Incorporation

### 14.1 Current Framework

MIDAO explicitly markets the Marshall Islands DAO LLC as suitable for AI agent incorporation. The framework supports this because:

- **No human managers required**: An algorithmically managed DAO LLC can operate with smart contracts as the sole governance mechanism.
- **Smart contract governance**: AI agents can operate through smart contracts without human intermediaries for routine decisions.
- **Legal personhood**: The DAO LLC can own property, enter contracts, and transact on behalf of the AI agent.
- **Treasury management**: Autonomous treasury operations are supported by the algorithmically managed model.

### 14.2 Limitations

- At least one UBO must still be identified (a human being).
- AML/KYC compliance requires human accountability.
- The implied covenant of good faith still applies to all members.
- Dispute resolution mechanisms require human participation.

## 15. Comparison with Wyoming DAO LLC

### 15.1 Feature Comparison

| Feature | Marshall Islands DAO LLC | Wyoming DAO LLC |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| **Sovereignty** | Sovereign nation, not subject to US federal law | US state, subject to US federal law and regulations |
| **Entity Type** | Non-profit and For-profit | For-profit only (pass-through) |
| **Taxation** | 0% (non-profit) or 3% GRT (for-profit) | Pass-through to members (taxed at individual rates, potentially high) |
| **Member Minimum** | None | None (but see quorum) |
| **Quorum Requirement** | Flexible (set by operating agreement) | 50% quorum required by law (impractical for large DAOs) |
| **Smart Contract Recognition** | Explicit statutory recognition | Limited recognition |
| **Directors/Officers** | Not required | Not required |
| **Privacy** | Only 25%+ holders identified | Subject to US disclosure requirements |
| **Securities Treatment** | Governance tokens explicitly not securities (if no economic rights) | Subject to SEC and state securities law |
| **Inactivity Dissolution** | No automatic dissolution | Dissolution if DAO fails to approve any proposals or take actions |
| **Open-Source Immunity** | Yes (2023 amendment) | No |
| **Series LLC** | Yes (2023 amendment) | No (but Wyoming offers separate Series LLC statute) |
| **Registration Timeline** | Max 30 days | Varies |
| **Legal Precedent** | Delaware precedent (by statute) | Wyoming state law |
| **US Regulatory Exposure** | Minimal | Full exposure to SEC, CFTC, IRS, FinCEN |
| **Banking Access** | Possible but challenging | Easier (US entity) |
| **Track Record** | 250+ protocols since 2022 | Limited adoption |

### 15.2 Why Choose Marshall Islands Over Wyoming

1. **No US federal regulatory exposure**: Incorporating in the US "increases your legal nexus there and makes you subject to more US laws & regulations." For token launches especially, avoiding US nexus is critical.
2. **Tax efficiency**: No pass-through taxation to members. Wyoming LLCs pass through to members at their individual rates.
3. **Governance flexibility**: No mandatory 50% quorum (which is impractical for most DAOs).
4. **Securities clarity**: Statutory governance token exemption vs. uncertain SEC treatment.
5. **No inactivity dissolution**: Wyoming dissolves DAOs that fail to act; RMI does not.
6. **Privacy**: Fewer disclosure requirements for members.
7. **Non-profit option**: Available from the start without separate IRS application.

### 15.3 Why Choose Wyoming Over Marshall Islands

1. **US banking**: Easier to open US bank accounts.
2. **Domestic recognition**: Better recognized by US counterparties.
3. **Established case law**: More precedent specifically for DAOs.
4. **No offshore stigma**: Avoids perception issues with offshore entities.
5. **For-profit simplicity**: Direct pass-through taxation may be simpler for small US-based DAOs.

## 18. Risks and Considerations

### 18.1 Jurisdictional Risks

- **TCMI Monopoly (2025)**: The Trust Company of the Marshall Islands holds exclusive authority over non-domestic formations. Can dissolve entities if it ceases providing registered agent services. This concentration of power is a risk.
- **Pre-Approval Shift**: TCMI now mandates pre-approval of activities before incorporation, making the jurisdiction more permissioned than previously.
- **Small Jurisdiction**: The Marshall Islands is a small Pacific island nation (population ~42,000). Political or economic instability could affect the regulatory environment.
- **COFA Dependency**: The Compact of Free Association with the US provides defense and economic support, but also creates dependency and potential for US influence on regulatory matters.

### 18.2 Banking Risks

- Banking access remains challenging and not guaranteed.
- Some financial institutions classify RMI as high-risk.
- Limited domestic banking infrastructure.
- Correspondent banking relationships are fragile.

### 18.3 Regulatory Risks

- **No VASP License Available**: The Marshall Islands currently does not offer VASP licenses, effectively making it illegal to operate a VASP (exchange, custody service) in the jurisdiction. This limits what DAO LLCs can do directly.
- **Evolving Regulations**: The framework is still new and evolving. The 2024 Regulations changed requirements, and further changes are likely.
- **Limited Case Law**: Despite following Delaware precedent, there is minimal RMI-specific DAO case law. Untested legal waters for edge cases.

### 18.4 Enforcement Risks

- RMI penalties are severe: $500/day for ongoing violations, up to $10,000 fines, potential imprisonment.
- Certificate of formation can be cancelled for non-compliance.
- Limited local legal infrastructure for complex disputes.

### 18.5 Perception Risks

- "Offshore" incorporation may raise concerns with institutional counterparties, investors, or regulators.
- Some jurisdictions view offshore structures with suspicion regardless of actual compliance.
- KYC/AML requirements help mitigate this but do not eliminate it.

## Appendix A: Operating Agreement Template Outline

Based on the Odos DAO LLC operating agreement (the most detailed publicly available example), a Marshall Islands DAO LLC operating agreement should contain:

### Article I: Organization
- Entity name and type (DAO LLC)
- Date of formation
- Governing statutes (LLC Act 1996, Non-Profit Act 2021, DAO Act 2022)
- Registered agent and office
- Purpose statement

### Article II: Membership
- Membership eligibility (token holding criterion)
- Specific smart contract address and blockchain
- Token standard (ERC-20, SPL, etc.)
- Single or multiple membership classes
- Voting rights (per-token or one-member-one-vote)
- Voting delegation mechanism
- Initial members designation

### Article III: Governance
- Governance programs (on-chain voting rules)
- Proposal types and approval thresholds
- Quorum requirements
- Special voting procedures
- Off-chain proposal interaction with on-chain voting

### Article IV: Management
- Member-managed or algorithmically managed designation
- Special Delegates (if any) — named individuals with specific powers
- Administrative authority delegation
- Councils and subgroups
- AML policy adoption authority

### Article V: Dissociation
- Automatic dissociation triggers (zero token balance)
- Restricted Person definition (sanctions lists)
- Voluntary withdrawal
- Involuntary removal

### Article VI: Liability and Fiduciary Duties
- No member liability for entity obligations
- Waiver of fiduciary duties
- Implied covenant of good faith
- Indemnification provisions

### Article VII: Non-Profit Restrictions (if applicable)
- Prohibition on profit distribution
- Prohibited activities
- Reasonable compensation provisions

### Article VIII: Amendments
- Amendment process (member vote via governance program)
- Electronic execution (wallet signatures)
- Smart contract upgrade procedures

### Article IX: Dissolution
- Dissolution triggers
- Liquidation procedures
- Asset distribution upon dissolution

### Article X: Dispute Resolution
- Mandatory negotiation period (30 days)
- Arbitration (International Centre for Dispute Resolution)
- Governing law (Marshall Islands)
- No court jurisdiction for agreement claims

### Article XI: Records
- On-chain Member Registry satisfies member list requirement
- Document retention requirements
- Confidentiality provisions

### Exhibits
- List of Special Delegates (names and roles)
- Smart contract addresses
- Governance program specifications
- Initial member designations
